resources

  • Eyes on Exoplanets


    • This interactive website allows people to visually explore the distribution of exoplanetary systems in three-dimensional space, view where the exoplanets are in our galaxy, learn what kinds of stars they orbit, and more.

  • Jet Propulsion Laboratory STEM Lessons


    This is a repository of educational materials and activities focused on teaching about exoplanets. Find lesson plans, activities, multimedia materials, and other resources that can be used to teach students about exoplanets and the science behind their discovery and study.

  • NASA Exoplanet Archive


    The NASA Exoplanet Archive is hosted by the Infrared Processing and Analysis Center (IPAC) at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech). This archive provides a wealth of data and resources for researchers, educators, and members of the general public who are interested in exoplanet science. Users can access information about individual exoplanetary systems, including the characteristics of the planets, their host stars, and the methods used for their discovery. The archive also offers tools for data visualization, analysis, and access to research papers and other related resources.
